# IntegrateWise — Brand Messaging System

Canonical messaging source for tagline hierarchy, approved copy by asset, Spine naming, and brand motifs.

---

## 1. Master Descriptor

**IntegrateWise**

**A Knowledge Workspace empowered by AI and the Spine — the unified intelligence layer that connects tools, context, and decisions.**

Use this as the default company/product descriptor in decks, brochures, one-pagers, and company-facing documents.

---

## 2. Tagline Hierarchy

### Primary Tagline

**AI Thinks in Context — and Waits for Approval**

### Short Version

**Context-Aware AI. Approval-Controlled Work.**

### Extended Marketing Line

**AI Thinks in Context. Humans Stay in Control. Every Action Waits for Approval.**

---

## 3. Product Essence

**IntegrateWise is a knowledge workspace where the Spine becomes the single source of truth and AI operates on top of that context — thinking, proposing, and learning while every action remains under human approval.**

Use this paragraph for:

- marketing copy
- company docs
- deck summaries
- product intros

---

## 4. Approved Spine Naming

Use these forms depending on the surface:

- **The Spine (SSOT)**
- **The Spine — Unified Intelligence Layer**
- **The Spine — Single Source of Truth and Unified Intelligence Layer**

### Guidance

- Use **The Spine (SSOT)** when brevity matters.
- Use **The Spine — Unified Intelligence Layer** in sales and marketing storytelling.
- Use **The Spine — Single Source of Truth and Unified Intelligence Layer** in strategy, product, and investor docs where both trust and category need to be explicit.

## 6. Messaging Guardrails

Always emphasize:

- the workspace
- the Spine as the unified intelligence layer
- AI operating on top of context
- human approval before action

For deck covers, it is acceptable to use the approved subtitle variant ending in **“Waits for Action”** while keeping the primary brand tagline as **“AI Thinks in Context — and Waits for Approval.”**

Do not reduce IntegrateWise to:

- a chatbot
- an automation engine
- a data migration product
- a warehouse or dashboard
- a Customer Success-only product

---

## 7. Brand Motifs

### Spine Node Line

Connected circles and lines inspired by the logo symbol. Use for:

- subtle backgrounds
- dividers
- topology-like decorative systems
- connection and flow metaphors

### Approval Checkpoint

Small rounded rectangle or node used as a visual approval stage marker. Use to represent:

- HITL
- approval gates
- governed action
- decision checkpoints

### Context Card

Soft-edged box representing structured knowledge and context blocks. Use for:

- entity summaries
- evidence cards
- workspace previews
- layered information blocks

### Layer Bands

Subtle layered strips suggesting:

- workspace layer
- intelligence layer
- governance layer

These should imply stacked system design without clutter.

---

## 8. Visual Aesthetic

### Enterprise Minimal + Intelligent Systems

Use this visual direction:

- trusted enterprise platform
- modern SaaS product
- structured intelligence system
- human-governed AI

Avoid this visual direction:

- flashy startup graphics
- old-fashioned corporate stationery
- crowded marketing layouts
- generic tech templates

### Design interpretation

The brand should feel:

- precise
- calm
- connected
- intelligent
- trustworthy
- structured

It should not feel:

- noisy
- overly playful
- futuristic for its own sake
- abstract without meaning

# IntegrateWise — Positioning and GTM

Canonical messaging for category, tagline, uniqueness, ICP, GTM wedge, and expansion path.

---

## 1. Positioning

### 1.1 Category

IntegrateWise is **a Knowledge Workspace empowered by AI and the Spine — the unified intelligence layer that connects tools, context, and decisions.**

It combines:

- an **adaptive Spine (SSOT)** for truth and context
- a **role-shaped workspace** for day-to-day work
- a **governed intelligence loop** for proposals, approvals, and action

### 1.2 Primary tagline

**AI Thinks in Context — and Waits for Approval**

### 1.3 Short version

**Context-Aware AI. Approval-Controlled Work.**

### 1.4 One-line positioning

**IntegrateWise turns fragmented systems into one Spine — the unified intelligence layer for truth, context, decisions, and governed AI.**

### 1.5 What IntegrateWise is not

- not a CRM replacement
- not a BI dashboard
- not an ETL or migration product
- not a generic AI copilot
- not a chatbot layered on top of partial context

### 1.6 Why this positioning wins

Most products solve one slice:

- system of record
- analytics
- automation
- AI assistant

IntegrateWise solves the missing layer between them:

- unified truth
- linked context
- operational workspace
- governed intelligence

## 2. Core Message

### 2.1 Main promise

**AI becomes useful only when it runs on connected truth.**

IntegrateWise gives teams:

- one connected view of the business
- one adaptive model across tools
- one place for signals, proposals, approvals, and action

### 2.2 Product promise

**AI thinks in context and waits for approval.**

That means:

- no direct AI truth writes
- no unsafe automation by default
- no recommendations without evidence
- no action without governance

## 3. Why the Spine matters

The Spine is the product moat and the unified intelligence layer.

It is:

- the **single source of truth and context**
- the **unified intelligence layer** across tools, context, and decisions
- the **identity layer** across systems
- the **memory layer** across time
- the **adaptive schema** that grows with the tenant

The Spine fits everyone because it is not a single department schema. It changes shape by:

- industry
- department
- connected systems
- observed schema growth
- goals and metrics

### 3.1 How the Spine fits different buyers

| Buyer | What they buy emotionally | What the Spine gives them |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Customer Success** | “Help me stop finding risk too late.” | One account truth across CRM, support, product, docs, and communication |
| **RevOps** | “Help me make the system coherent.” | One model across pipeline, routing, forecasting, attribution, and hygiene |
| **Sales** | “Help me see the whole account and next move.” | Unified account, deal, stakeholder, and activity context |
| **Support** | “Help me resolve issues with full history.” | Connected ticket, customer, and evidence context |
| **Finance / Ops** | “Help me reduce workflow fragmentation.” | Unified contract, billing, obligation, and approval context |
| **Leadership** | “Help me know what changed and what matters.” | Cross-functional truth with signals and pending actions |

## 4. Uniqueness

### 4.1 Product uniqueness

IntegrateWise is unique because it combines:

1. **Adaptive Spine**
   One SSOT that grows by tenant, rather than one hardcoded schema or many disconnected schemas.

2. **Three trust-aware flows**
   Structured, unstructured, and AI-generated content do not share the same trust path.

3. **Workspace and intelligence on the same model**
   The same Spine that stores truth also shapes the workspace and powers AI.

4. **Governed action loop**
   Think -> Govern -> HITL -> Act -> Adjust is built into the core product, not bolted on.

5. **No rip-and-replace motion**
   IntegrateWise creates value across the existing stack instead of demanding migration first.

### 4.2 Strategic uniqueness

Most AI platforms:

- reason on partial data
- lack entity-level grounding
- do not separate truth from AI-generated content
- automate without a hard approval model

IntegrateWise is differentiated by:

- **truth before intelligence**
- **context before automation**
- **approval before execution**
- **compounding knowledge over time**

## 5. GTM Wedge

### 5.1 Best initial wedge

**Start with account-centric teams in B2B SaaS and tech-enabled companies, especially Customer Success and RevOps.**

### 5.2 Why this wedge works

- high pain from fragmented systems
- easy-to-understand value story
- clear measurable outcomes
- natural cross-functional expansion path
- strong proof narrative around churn, renewals, expansion, and account health

### 5.3 Ideal customer profile

- B2B SaaS or tech-enabled business
- multiple systems already in use: CRM, support, docs, communication, analytics
- account-based revenue model
- post-sales complexity or RevOps complexity
- decision-maker who already feels AI is underpowered because context is fragmented

### 5.4 Landing use case

Land with one urgent use case:

- account risk visibility
- renewal and expansion prioritization
- one account 360 with evidence
- governed next-best-actions

### 5.5 Expansion path

1. Land in Customer Success / RevOps
2. Expand to Sales and Support
3. Extend into Product, Finance, and leadership workflows
4. Expand from one domain workspace into broader operating system usage
